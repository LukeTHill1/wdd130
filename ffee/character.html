Characters Information

                This page will be dedicated to the different characters that are given in the mainline and DLC content. This page will start with a few explanations about character skills, classes, their stats, and how they grow. Then there will be a report of each character consisting of a general explanation of they are, their pros and cons, and how they can be made to stay useful throughout the entire game until the final chapter.
	
	            In Fire Emblem Engage, characters are central to both the story and gameplay, each offering unique abilities, personalities, and growth opportunities. Here’s a breakdown of how characters work, including their roles, progression, and roles:

Starting Classes

Each character begins in a specific class that determines their initial abilities, stats, weapon proficiencies, and general role on the battlefield. Classes include traditional roles like Sword Fighter, Archer, Mage, Cavalier, and Martial Monk.

Class Promotions

Characters can upgrade or change classes through a system of promotions. Using Master Seals or Second Seals, players can advance characters to stronger versions of their base class when they hit level 10 (like Sword Fighter to Swordmaster) or switch them to other classes to fit their playstyle or strategy.

Advanced Classes

Once promoted, characters can access new abilities, weapons, and improved stats. Some advanced classes have unique skills that enhance a character’s effectiveness and versatility.

Core Stats

Characters have core stats that affect their performance in combat. These include:
HP (Health Points): Determines how much damage a character can take.

Strength: Affects physical attack power.

Magic: Influences the power of magic attacks.

Dexterity: Affects hit rate and critical hit chance.

Speed: Determines Attack Speed (AS) and ability to double-attack or avoid being double-attacked.

Defense: Reduces damage taken from physical attacks.

Resistance: Reduces damage taken from magical attacks.

Build: Affects how well a character can wield heavy weapons without a speed penalty.

Luck: Provides minor boosts to hit rate, dodge rate, and critical avoid rate.

Growth Rates

Each character has unique growth rates for these stats. These determine how likely each stat is to increase when the character levels up. Some characters may excel in physical stats, while others have higher growth in magic or speed.

Emblem Rings

Emblem Rings let characters “Engage” with legendary heroes from past Fire Emblem games. When equipped with an Emblem Ring, a character can merge with the hero’s spirit, gaining access to Engage Skills, special abilities, and stat boosts.

Bonds Levels

Each character can build a Bond Level with Emblems by fighting while equipped with their ring. As Bond Levels increase, the character unlocks additional skills and stat boosts, enhancing their capabilities in combat.

Bonds Rings

These are simpler rings that provide minor stat bonuses but don’t allow characters to Engage. Bond Rings can be created using Bond Fragments and offer collectible, stackable benefits for characters who don’t have an Emblem Ring.

Class Skills

Each class comes with unique skills that characters automatically gain by being in that class. These skills provide bonuses or abilities suited to the class role. For example, a healer class might have a skill that boosts healing effectiveness.

Inheritable Skills

As characters build Bond Levels with Emblems, they can inherit certain skills permanently. This allows for further customization, as players can tailor skills to make characters more versatile and better suited to specific combat situations.

Personal Skills

Each character also has a unique personal skill that reflects their personality or role in the story. These skills are active regardless of class and provide bonuses or situational advantages. For example, a character might have a personal skill that grants a bonus when near allies or that boosts their damage under certain conditions.

Leveling Up and Stat Gains

Characters gain experience and level up through combat, with random stat gains based on growth rates. Leveling up is essential for improving a character’s stats and preparing them for class promotion.

Class Change with Seals

By using Master Seals (for promotions) and Second Seals (for changing classes), players can explore different character roles, allowing flexibility and experimentation with builds. This can help optimize a character’s strengths or balance out weaknesses.

Somniel Activities

At the Somniel, characters can engage in activities like working out, eating meals, or sparring, which provide temporary buffs and improve Support Levels. These activities allow players to boost stats or gain minor advantages before entering a battle.

Styles

Battle Styles are a type of attribute assigned to all units. Each grants a unique bonus or ability. In general, the Battle Style a unit has matches up with the type of unit they are, such as:
Dragon: Gain additional effects when Engaged with Emblems.

Backup: Will engage in Chain Attacks if they are in the range of an enemy being attacked by a player unit.

Covert: Receive doubled terrain bonus effects.

Mystical: Attack magic ignores enemy terrain bonuses.

Qi Adept: Can protect adjacent allies with Chain Guard if their HP is full.

Cavalry: Have a higher movement than other units.

Armored: Immunity to Break.

Flying: Unencumbered by terrain.

Who is Alear?

Background

Alear is an Avatar(character with player-defined traits), as well as the main character of Engage.
They are forced to be used on every main story map, and their death causes a game over.

In Chapter 11, Alear must be brought to the escape point.
1,000 years before the events of Engage, Alear, a divine dragon, fell into a deep slumber after the defeat of the Fell Dragon.
They wake up after 1,000 years to find the seal on the Fell Dragon weakening, with its reawakening imminent.

personality

Alear's memory is hazy due to having amnesia but they retains a spirit of positivity and courteousness.
Polite as one can expect of a Divine Dragon, Alear warmly greets and interacts with the members of their army and the people of Elyos.

Though they lost their memories of Lumera, Alear quickly bonds with her and adopts many of their mother's mannerisms.

Their journey tests their convictions, and even when near death, Alear refuses to give up and finds rather unorthodox solutions using the powers of their allies.
Despite their status as a Divine Dragon, Alear does not force people to address them with formalities and their title, preferring that they simply refer to them by name if they want to.
At times they are uncomfortable being the object of worship, especially when done in their presence.

Nonetheless, they are touched by the people's faith in them and fiercely protective of Elyos itself and its denizens.

Personal skill: Divinely Inspiring

Adjacent Allies deal +3 damage and take 1 less damage.

Pros

Alear is a strong character even in the first few chapters, it is very hard to take their hit points down to zero.
Their Dragon child class is the “Lord” class that every protagonist in the Fire Emblem games has and in regular fashion with their regular deployment makes them the highest level character.

-Alear has a good enough mix of dexterity and speed that allows them to do follow-up attacks each time they initiate combat which gives their average strength a chance to capitalize on the multiple attacks they can do in a turn.

-With their battle style of Dragon, they can pretty much wield any Emblem with every little drawback except taking it from another unit that might need it more and giving the engage skills/attacks a consistent addition effect or effects depending on the Emblem.

-When they advance to their advanced class Divine Dragon which gives the ability to wield the Marshel Arts covering their low resistance stat and giving them an advantage against bows, tomes, and staves.

Cons

Just because they are the main character, does not mean they are the perfect unit and they do have some flaws that can be exploited by the enemies.

-Their resistance is low enough that you would have to be worried about them being surrounded by magic users, especially with their low weapon range until Chapter 25 when they get the Wille Glanz but that’s two chapters away from the end of the game.

-Even though they don’t use their magic stat with their classes, with how low it gets it does keep him from using emblems that focus on the magic stat.

-His attack stat is average which leaves his attacks bouncing off of armored units in the earlier chapters without an Armorslayer.

How to make them useful

As they stand, the most that you as the player can do for their stats is to give them a few tailsmen and maybe an energy drop for some extra strength, along with keeping a set of resistance tonics on hand if needed.
On the other hand, for their emblems, there are a lot of options to pick from so I will sum it down to the two that will help him survive the longest:

Marth: This is the basic answer with his increases to Avoid and the Sword Agility skills gained from bond levels, along with Unyielding that restores hp when they go below a certain percentage and Break Defenses that give an extra attack after inflicting Break.
This is a strong choice especially when paired with skills like Ike’s Resolve which boosts defense and resistance if their HP stays under 75 percent, Roy’s Hold out which allows the unit to survive with 1 HP if they have a certain HP at the start of combat, and or Lyn’s Alacrity which allows them to do their follow up attack before the foe can counter attack.

And if you have access to the Emblem Bracelets then look at skills like weapon sync from the triplets or Brute force from Chrom & Robin.

Corrin: This is my personal choice with her increases in HP and the Quality time skill that restores HP to adjacent allies creating an interesting synergy with their Personal skill Divinvely Inspiring, along with being able to access all the dragon veins that she has available that give special effects to the terrain and Draconic Hex that lowers all the foes stats by 4 shrinking by 1 each turn.
This gives them a large HP pool to take hits from even magic users and can be made stronger when paired with skills like Marth’s Perception which gives a boost to Avoid when the unit starts combat and increases with higher speed stats, Ike’s Resolve which boosts defense and resistance if their HP stays under 75 percent, Roy’s Hold out which allows the unit to survive with 1 HP if they have a certain HP at the start of combat, and or Lyn’s Alacrity which allows them to do their follow up attack before the foe can counter attack.

And if you have access to the Emblem Bracelets then look at skills like weapon sync from the triplets, Brute force from Chrom & Robin, or Geosphere/Lifesphere/Lightsphere from Tiki if you want to boost the synergy with Quality Time and their Personal skill.
